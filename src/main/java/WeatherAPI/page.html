Did You Know?

Snakes don't have eyelids! Instead of blinking, they have a thin, transparent scale called a "brille" that protects their eyes. This gives them a constant, unblinking stare that can be quite unnerving if you're a mouse.

Ever wonder why snakes flick their tongues? They're actually "smelling" the air! They pick up chemical signals and deliver them to the Jacobson's organ in the roof of their mouth to figure out what's nearby.

The Barbados threadsnake is the smallest snake in the world, measuring only about 4 inches long. It's so thin it could crawl through a pencil if it were hollow! It mostly eats termite larvae.

Not all snakes lay eggs! While about 70% of snakes are oviparous (egg-layers), many species like vipers and garter snakes are viviparous, meaning they give birth to live young. Evolution is wild!
